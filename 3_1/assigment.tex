\section*{Comments}

d. The first is the advection caused by the wind in x direction.
The second is the advection in y direction, which is zero for our case
as we have no coriolis effect. The third is the movment produced by the
shape of the water.

f, g. The total mass seems to be conserved, as the waves can be seen increasing 
and combining their masses as they collide. As such the mass is conserved.
Still, the wave starts to act strangely as they pass each other, but this seems 
to be the expected behaviour.
